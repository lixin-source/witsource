Providing Resources and Opportunity
Women and non-binary computer scientists are important to the technology industry. However, there is a lack of support for these groups of people within academia and the workforce.

18 US technology conferences revealed that, on average, only 27 percent of keynote or standalone speakers were female

Source

72% of women in tech have worked at a company where bro culture is pervasive. That average includes 83% of women in sales, 80% of women in marketing, and 63% of women in IT/engineering. Source

Women in tech are 4X more likely than men to see gender bias as an obstacle to promotion. 39% of women see gender bias as a barrier to promotion in 2021.Source

WiTSource provides a centralized guide for women and non-binary people to find resources for upper level computer science classes, opportunities, and more.
